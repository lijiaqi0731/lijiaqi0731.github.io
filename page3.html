REASEARCH

From 1990 to 2007, South Africa lost an average of 13 rhinos to poaching each year; today, around 3 are lost there every day.Although South Africa’s Kruger National Park has historically borne the brunt of this poaching onslaught, official figures since 2015 have revealed a steady, if marginal, decrease in illegal rhino killing within the park—2015 statistics showed 70% of rhinos poached in the country occurred in Kruger, compared to 60% in 2017.

COMPETITOR

World Animal Protection

This organization is trying to help people know more about animals, and move them to protect animals. Their purpose has 5 points. First they end the needless suffering of animals. Secondly, they want to influence decision makers to put animals on the global agenda. Next, they help the world see how important animals are to all of us. Forth, they inspire people to change animals’ lives for the better. Last, they move the world to protect animals.
